Interactive Eye Anatomy

Explore the complex and beautiful structure of the human eye. Hover over the parts to learn more.

Cornea

The clear front surface of the eye. It acts like a camera lens, focusing light as it enters the eye.

Iris

The colored part of the eye that controls the size of the pupil to regulate light.

Lens

A transparent structure behind the iris that helps focus light directly onto the retina.

Retina

The light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye that converts light into electrical signals.

Optic Nerve

The bundle of nerve fibers that carries visual information from the retina to the brain.
